FAQ

Straight answers about selling instantly.

Everything you'd want to know before you place your first NFT. Still curious? Open the app — seeing a price costs nothing and moves nothing.

How can a sale actually be instant?
Traditional selling waits for a buyer. Sell NFT doesn't. The appraisal engine prices your NFT against comparable sales, the floor, live bids and its traits, then stands behind a firm offer you can take immediately. Accepting triggers a single atomic swap that settles in seconds.
Do you ever hold or take custody of my NFT?
No. Sell NFT is non-custodial. Your NFT stays in your wallet until the exact moment you accept an offer, when it swaps atomically for funds. There is no deposit step and no intermediary holding your asset.
Do I need a wallet just to see a price?
No. You can drop an image into the appraisal slot and get an estimate with no wallet connected. You only connect a wallet when you want to sell a specific token you own and actually settle.
How is the offer priced?
From live market signals: recent comparable sales, the collection floor, standing bids, and the item's traits and rarity. Those form a fair-value estimate; a small, visible instant-liquidity spread is subtracted to produce the offer you can take right now. The breakdown is shown, never hidden.
Which NFTs and collections are supported?
Anything with a real market — from blue-chip collections to long-tail pieces. If comparable sales and bids exist for it, the engine can quote it. Thinly traded items get a wider, clearly-marked confidence range.
What do I get paid in, and are there fees?
Payouts settle in the native asset of the chain (e.g. ETH), straight to your wallet. Getting an appraisal is free and costs no gas. The only cost is the transparent instant-liquidity spread already reflected in the offer — there are no listing or cancellation fees.
Can I decline the offer?
Always. An offer is an option, not an obligation. If the number isn't for you, walk away — nothing moves, nothing is listed, and there's no fee for looking.
How long is an offer valid?
Offers are quoted against live market data and refresh as that data moves, so a quote holds for a short window. Accepting locks the price you see for settlement of that transaction.
Is this financial advice or a guaranteed price?
No. Appraisals are estimates from on-chain market data, not financial advice, and market prices can move. The offer you accept is firm for that settlement — but decisions and risk are yours.
